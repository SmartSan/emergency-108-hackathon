About

Organised by TNHSP and IIT Madras, The Emergency Services Application Hackathon brings you the opportunity to innovate and have a positive impact on the current structure of the 108 Emergency Services in Tamil Nadu .

In case of any emergency, rapid intervention is critical in determining the outcome. The emergency service personnel have the crucial responsibility of bringing the response time to as minimal as possible.

And Innovations in technology can play an integral part in achieving this goal. The aim of this hackathon is to come up with solutions that leverage the capabilities of a smart phone and integrate them with the backend infrastructure at the Call-Centre to make the flow of information fast and seamless thereby greatly increasing the efficiency.

Theme

The theme / problem statement for this hackathon is to develop an applicaiton that will help 108 Emergency Services to locate the caller, and dispatch as quickly as possible.

The solution should at least have the following aspects with the given requirements

Smartphone App
  • Relays the geo-location of caller/user to the call centre
  • Provide information about nearby hospitals and blood banks to user, even in offline mode

Web App
  • Simulates the backend infrastructure at the call centre
  • Access database of the locations of all ambulances and hospitals in Tamil Nadu
  • Logs information about every call to a database and dispatches call to nearest ambulance
  • Provides an API, to access data stored in the application by other services
Developing algorithms for analytics on the data collected is encouraged. Any other aspect of innovation above and beyond the aforementioned, in the theme of emergency services are appreciated.

FAQ

What is Emergency Services Hackathon?

The Emergency Services Hackathon is aimed at developing solutions for a quick and efficient response to emergencies. This is sponsored by the Tamil Nadu Government and aims to incorporate the winning solutions in the real field

What should I build?

The solutions should create a smart phone app that uses location data to decrease the time of response for the accident site. This will also involve creating a web service application to simulate the 108 Centre and also log in the data to leverage the benefits of data analytics.

How big can my team be?

The teams can be anywhere between 1-3 and should have current students as participants. If you have any queries please use the information provided under contact us.

Can I use external APIs, services and SDKs?

You are encouraged to use any 3rd party services, APIs, open source projects, libraries, and frameworks as long as they are publicly available on the web and do not infringe on any copyrights .

What is the structure of the event?

There will be a preliminary round involving online submissions and the shortlisted teams will be brought down to IITM for a one day hackathon event where the teams will be allowed to showcase their work to the panel of experts. The online submissions should provide details of the solution such as innovative aspects in the teams approach, progress etc. in the form of a word document. Any pictures and screenshots should can also be inserted in the word document .The Timeline for this is available in the website.

Are Travel Reimbursements provided for shortlisted teams?

Yes, the shortlisted teams will be provided a travel allowance to cover all/a portion of their travel expenses subject to a cap on the maximum reimbursement provided.

What is the Prize Money?

The total prize money for the winning teams is 2,00,000. They are divided as follows:
First Place - INR 1,00,000
Second Place - INR 60,000
Third Place - INR 40,000
